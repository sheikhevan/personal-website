For a long time now, I have been fascinated with the idea of polymathy. A polymath is defined by [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polymath) as "an individual whose knowledge spans many different subjects". While the contemporary trend is to be a specialist, usually encouraged by a the career centered lifestyle of so many people , a polymath chooses instead to be good at many different things. But how do I become one?

## Learning how to learn

The first and most important step in becoming a polymath is learning how to learn. This is an area which I am still learning how to do myself, though I'll share what I've learned so far. The most important thing is to take notes, it's important to take notes physically on a piece of paper instead of digitally. A [study](https://www.frontiersin.org/articles/10.3389/fpsyg.2023.1219945/full) measured the brain waves of students who wrote by hand compared to students who took notes digitally and found that there was increased electrical activity across the brain regions responsible for sensory processing and memory.

## Necessary skills

What are the essential skills a prospective polymath must have? I would say to be a polymath you have to have physical, mental, and artistic skills. A physical skill would be something like bike riding, sports, or martial arts. Mental skills are more varied, as well as more essential. Some mental skills are geography, history, philosophy (this one's important), and mathematics. Finally, artistic skills are things like writing, poetry, and painting.

## Acquiring skills

But how do I acquire these skills? Well, the good news is the high school curriculum (at least in the US) already has lots of skills in it, to the point where the average high schooler would know way more than most people 100 years ago ever would. For other things not covered in high school, the answer is simple, practice and reading. Practice is critical for what I hope should be obvious reasons. Reading allows you to access the knowledge of another persons brain at a fraction of a cost it took them to develop that knowledge. Great polymaths are also great readers.

## Conclusion
Thats it for today, the journey towards becoming a polymath is both an exciting and challenging endeavor. I wish you luck on your road to polymathy.
